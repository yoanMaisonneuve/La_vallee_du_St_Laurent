# Global Plan
## Version 1.2 · April 2026
## The Reference Document for Civilizational Direction

---

## Why This Document Exists

Every podcast, every interview, every conversation about the future starts from scratch.
Someone asks: *"Where are we going?"* and we answer from memory, from feeling, from the mood of the day.

There is no point of reference.
No shared map.
No document you can open and say: *"Here. This is where we are. This is where we are going. This is the plan."*

This document fixes that.

It is not a prediction. It is a **direction**.
It is not complete. It is a **living reference** — updated as the world moves.
It is not for one person. It is for anyone who wants to talk about the plan with numbers, with stages, with honesty about what we don't know yet.

---

## The Core Thesis

> We are at the beginning of the largest transition in human history.
> The question is not whether it happens. The question is whether we manage it — or it manages us.

The transition is happening across every field simultaneously. That has never happened before. Every previous revolution — agricultural, industrial, digital — happened in one domain at a time. This one is happening everywhere at once.

That is both the opportunity and the risk.

The plan is to manage this transition **intentionally** — with direction, with measurement, with human-AI collaboration at the center of execution.

### 1. Human-AI Collaboration
**The meta-field. Everything else depends on this.**

**Where we are:**
- AI is used as a search engine by most people
- ~5% of users build systems with AI, 95% send single prompts
- The bottleneck is not compute — it is human context quality
- 80-90% of agent projects fail in production (RAND 2025) — cause: bad context

**Where we are going:**
- Every knowledge worker has an AI co-worker within 5 years
- AI handles execution, humans handle vision and validation
- New literacy: prompt engineering → system design → agent orchestration

**The Plan:**
- Phase 1 (2026–2027): Establish protocols. Enter-game model. Solo founders first.
- Phase 2 (2027–2029): Small teams adopt human-AI hybrid workflows
- Phase 3 (2029–2032): Institutional adoption. Governments, schools, hospitals.
- Phase 4 (2032+): Native AI integration. New generation builds AI-first by default.

**Tasks for AI:** Context management, execution, documentation, monitoring, pattern detection
**Tasks for humans:** Vision, validation, ethics, relationships, creative direction, final decisions

**Bottlenecks:**
| Bottleneck | Level | Description |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| Context quality | Critical | Humans don't know how to give AI the right context |
| Trust gap | High | People don't trust AI outputs enough to act on them |
| Interface bandwidth | High | Voice/text is slow — neural interfaces 5–10 years away |
| Regulatory lag | Medium | Laws written for pre-AI world |
| Education gap | Medium | Most people have no framework for working with AI |

## The Human Adoption Bottleneck
**The most critical section of this document. Everything else is theory until this is solved.**

---

### Why This Section Exists Separately

Every field in this plan ultimately depends on one thing: **humans choosing to engage.**

You can build the best AI tutor. The best diagnostic tool. The best clean energy grid. The best urban management system.

If humans don't adopt — nothing moves.

And right now, human adoption is the single biggest bottleneck across every field simultaneously. Not technology. Not funding. Not regulation.

**People.**

---

### The Adoption Chain — How It Actually Works

This is the real sequence. Not the one in the textbooks. The one that actually happens.

```
New technology appears
        ↓
Person hears about it (awareness)
        ↓
Person feels threatened or curious (emotional response — not rational)
        ↓
Person tries it once (first contact)
        ↓
[THE VALLEY OF DESPAIR]
Person struggles, feels incompetent, questions why they're doing this
        ↓
80% drop out here
        ↓
20% push through → find first value moment
        ↓
Person tells someone they trust (social proof)
        ↓
That person tries — cycle repeats
        ↓
Critical mass → cultural shift → new normal
```

**The valley of despair is where revolutions die.**
Not from lack of technology. From lack of support at the moment of struggle.

---

### The Four Prerequisites for Human Adoption

Before a person can adopt any new system — AI, clean energy, new governance model — four things must be true. In order. They cannot be skipped.

```
1. SAFETY     → "I am not going to be destroyed by this"
2. TRUST      → "Someone I respect has done this and it worked"
3. RELEVANCE  → "This solves a problem I actually have right now"
4. CAPABILITY → "I can do this — I have been shown how"
```

**Most adoption programs start at 4. They fail because 1, 2, and 3 are not in place.**

Education programs fail because they teach capability before establishing safety.
Government campaigns fail because they communicate relevance before building trust.
Products fail because they assume trust that was never earned.

---

### The Self-Reinforcing Failure Loop

The mental health crisis (Field 14) and the adoption bottleneck are not separate problems. They are the same problem expressing itself in two domains.

```
AI transition accelerates
        ↓
Jobs become uncertain → identity threatened → anxiety rises
        ↓
Mental health deteriorates
        ↓
Cognitive bandwidth shrinks — person cannot learn new systems
        ↓
Person falls further behind in adoption
        ↓
Gap between them and AI-augmented peers widens
        ↓
Anxiety worsens → mental health deteriorates further
        ↓
[LOOP]
```

**This loop is already running at scale in 2026.** It is invisible in most adoption metrics because we measure technology deployment, not human readiness.

Breaking the loop requires intervening at the **mental health layer first** — not the technology layer.

---

### This Plan as a Companion — Not a Document

A document does not create engagement. A relationship does.

This global plan is designed to function as a **companion** — something a person, a company, a government can return to repeatedly and find themselves in it. Not as a spectator of the plan but as a participant in it.

**What that means in practice:**

| Old model | Companion model |
|-----------|----------------|
| Plan is presented to people | People find themselves in the plan |
| Experts hold the direction | Direction is shared and navigable by anyone |
| Progress is reported top-down | Progress is visible and trackable by every actor |
| Adoption is measured at scale | Adoption is felt at the individual level |
| The plan is static | The plan reflects reality as it changes |

---

### How to Use This Plan to Create Engagement

#### With an individual

**Goal:** Move them from spectator to participant in one conversation.

1. Ask: *"Which of these fields is most present in your life right now?"*
2. Show them where that field is on the phase map
3. Name the bottleneck closest to their situation
4. Ask: *"What would it mean for you if that bottleneck moved one level down?"*
5. Give them one concrete action that connects to the transition
6. Come back to the same document next time — show them the delta

The key: **they must see themselves in the map.** Abstract plans create observers. Personal maps create actors.

#### With a company

1. Map their business against the field table — which fields do they touch?
2. Show them which phase those fields are in
3. Show them the trigger conditions for the next phase
4. Ask: *"What does your company need to be when that trigger fires?"*
5. Build the internal plan backward from that moment

#### With a government

1. Show them the geopolitical dimension table — where is their country vs peers?
2. Identify which fields are in Disruption in their jurisdiction right now
3. Name the bottleneck that is costing them the most
4. Show them the implementation control layer — which level is their mandate?
5. Give them the metric that will move if they act

#### In a podcast or public conversation

### The Three Phases of Any Revolution

```
Phase 1: EMERGENCE    → Technology exists. Few know it. Pioneers use it.
Phase 2: DISRUPTION   → Technology spreads. Old systems break. Chaos.
Phase 3: INTEGRATION  → New normal established. Next revolution begins.
```

### Phase Trigger Conditions

A field moves from one phase to the next when specific conditions are met. These are the signals to watch.

| Transition | Trigger conditions |
|------------|-------------------|
| Emergence → Disruption | Adoption crosses 10% of target population OR cost drops 10x OR major institution adopts publicly |
| Disruption → Integration | Adoption crosses 50% OR regulatory framework passed OR incumbent industry restructures around new model |

### The Compounding Risk

When multiple fields are in Disruption simultaneously, the chaos compounds.
A health system under disruption + an economy under disruption + a political system under disruption = cascading instability.

**This is where we are in 2026.**

The plan is not to slow the revolutions. It is to sequence the integrations.

Each revolution should take less time than the previous one. This is the meta-goal.

## Geopolitical Dimension
**The same revolution, different speeds, different values.**

The transition is global but not uniform. Three major blocs are moving at different speeds with different priorities.

| Bloc | Approach | Strengths | Risks |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **USA** | Market-led, fast, fragmented regulation | Speed, capital, talent concentration | Inequality, no social safety net for transition |
| **China** | State-directed, coordinated, controlled | Scale, speed, infrastructure investment | Authoritarian use of AI, limited individual rights |
| **EU** | Rights-first, regulated, slower | Trust, fairness, democratic legitimacy | Regulatory overreach slowing innovation |
| **Global South** | Leapfrogging model, mobile-first | Can skip legacy infrastructure | Risk of becoming AI-dependent on other blocs |
| **Canada/Nordics** | Hybrid model — social safety net + innovation | Balance of speed and equity | Small population limits global influence |

**The risk:** AI development fractures into incompatible ecosystems — like internet splinternet but for intelligence.
**The opportunity:** The first bloc to demonstrate AI-augmented human flourishing at scale sets the model the world copies.

## How to Talk About This Plan

### In a podcast or interview

When someone asks *"where are we going?"* — open this document. Then say:

1. **Name the field** — which revolution are we talking about?
2. **State where we are** — use the metrics table
3. **State where we're going** — use the phase roadmap
4. **Name the bottleneck** — be specific, name the level
5. **Name who does what** — human tasks vs AI tasks
6. **Give the number** — how many countries, companies, people are here vs there

### The 3 questions that structure every conversation

1. *What phase is this field in right now?*
2. *What is the biggest bottleneck and what level is it?*
3. *What does the transition from here to the next phase require?*

### With a government official

1. Which field is their mandate?
2. What phase is that field in their country vs globally?
3. What is the trigger condition for the next phase?
4. What does government need to do vs what AI can do?
5. What is the cost of delay? (use metrics — X years behind = Y impact)

### With a company or investor

1. Which fields does their business touch?
2. Are they in Emergence, Disruption, or Integration?
3. What is the bottleneck they are closest to solving?
4. Where on the metrics table does their product move the needle?

### With an individual

1. Which field is their daily life most affected by right now?
2. What phase are they personally in — do they have an AI system or not?
3. What is the one thing they can do today to move from Emergence to Disruption personally?
